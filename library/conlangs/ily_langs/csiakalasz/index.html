Csiakalász language (10% complete)

Csiakalász [ˈtʃi.a.kɒˌlaːs] is an East Ilytharian language spoken in Csiakalésza. A large community of Csiakalász speakers can also be found in Qāchankētu. Csiakalász is also the language that borrowed a lot of its characteristics to Nyászokeányi, an IAL (Ilytharian Auxiliary Language).

__________________________________________________________________________________

History and Classification

Classification:

  • Ilytharian
    • Continental Ilytharian
      • Eastern continental Ilytharian
        • Csiakalász

Csiakalász separated itself from Hwêledu around 510 AD. That was the time when Hwêledu lost its uvular plosives, while Csiakalász kept the voiceless one. Csiakalász also kept both palatal plosive, though losing distinction between labialized and plain palatal/velar plosives.

There is a mild amount of dialectal variation. The most notable dialects are the Liacsára dialect, which is the standard form of the language as described in this article, and the Qāchankētu dialect. The Qāchankētu dialect sometimes realizes /c/ as [tɕ].

Phonology

Csiakalász probably has the most average Ilytharian phonology. There is one uvular and two palatal plosives, as well as the two dental fricatives and the two velar fricatives, but no lateral fricative, which is present in 3 out of 7 Ilytharian languages.

Irregularly spelt diphthongs

Diphthongs are generally possible with every combination of two vowels. Irregular ones are:

  • eÿ /eə/
  • aï /ai/
  • eö /eo/

Stressed vowels

Stressed vowels are pronounced long and stressed. Sometimes stressed vowels are pronounced using a different phoneme than their unstressed counterparts.

Unstressed vowel Stressed vowel
a /ɒ/ á /aː/
ai /æ/ ái /æː/
e /ɛ/ é /eː/
o /ɔ/ ó /oː/
u /ʊ/ ú /uː/

Vowel groups

Vowels are grouped into a high and a low group. These are not explicit vowel harmony, as high and low vowel can be used in the same word, but they will become important in the grammar section. Every high vowel has its low counterpart.

High vowel i ı y e é w ai ái
Low vowel ú u ey o ó eo a á

Vowel nasalization

Just like in Hwêledu, vowels are sometimes nasalized. This only applies to the vowels a, e and o in their unstressed variants.

Writing Systems

Currently, 4 main writing systems are in use:

  • Latin script (Látdinách qászá)
  • Standard Csiakalász Script (Paiqászá)
  • Quickscript (Vaizqászá)
  • Cursive script (Nyeozvqászá)

Latin script (Látdinách qászá)

The latin script is the third script to be introduced to the Csiakalász language. It was officially adopted as a main writing system around 480 AD.

Standard Csiakalász script (Paiqászá)

The standard Csiakalász script (Paiqászá) is the original abugida writing system of the language. The oldest writing dates back to c.1030 BC.

Quickscript (Vaizqászá)

Quickscript is a syllabary script that has been invented in 1674 by a scholar from Cwêda. It was adopted as an official writing system in 1679 (1678 in Hwêleducâ) and has been in use ever since due to its compactness, ease of writing and speed; on average, one Quickscript letter equals 3.9 Paiqászá letters (4.4 Latin letters).

Cursive script (Nyeozvqászá)

The cursive script is a featural script and the second oldest writing system. It is not only used by Csiakalász, but by all Ilytharian languages. Most of the time, it is used for ceremonial purposes, but sometimes also on other occasions.
