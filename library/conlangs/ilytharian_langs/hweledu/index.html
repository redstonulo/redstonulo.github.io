Hwêledu (Hwêledu: ['ʰwelɛðy]) is an East Ilytharian language spoken in Hwêleda. It is the official language there, as well as recognized minority languge in Csiakalész and Kymýsa. It is often said that Hwêledu has the simplest grammar out of all Ilytharian languages.

History and Classification

Classification:

  • Ilytharian
    • East Ilytharian
      • Hwêledu

Hwêledu diverged from Csiakalász at around 510 AD. It lost all uvular consonants, as well as palatal plosives. Later, in the time of Old Hwêledu, palatal plosives were regained through a shift from /t d/ to /c ɟ/. Consequently, there are no alveolar plosives in the language. It is also notable that Hwêledu has lost the least vowels when evolving from Proto-Ilytharian. There are 16 vowels present in the language, with no length or stress distinction whatsoever.

There are almost no dialects of Hwêledu. The only thing that varies by region is the pronunciation of /c ɟ/, which might be realized as /t d/ or /ʈ ɖ/ by some southern Hwêledu speakers.

Phonology

Hwêledu's phonology is a rather unique one for Ilytharian standards. It lacks uvular and alveolar plosives, which all other Ilytharian languages have. It is also the only language that distincts between the voiced and voiceless variant of the glottal fricative.

This consonant inventory is the most unique one out of all Ilytharian languages. The lack of alveolar and uvular plosives, no clear distinction between /ɣ/ and /x/, and distinction between voiced and voiceless glottal fricative are all very uncommon features for Ilytharian languages.
